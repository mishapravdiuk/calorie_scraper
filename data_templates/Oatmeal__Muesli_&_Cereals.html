Oatmeal, Muesli & Cereals Calories

Oatmeal is often a main ingredient in cereals like muesli and granola. Also called rolled oats or old-fashioned oats, plain oatmeal has high nutritional value because it offers lots of complex carbohydrates, some dietary fiber, and significant amounts of vitamin A, vitamin B6, and calcium. One cup of cooked oatmeal also provides 77% of the suggested daily value of iron. As with oatmeal, the calories in these products are primarily in the form of carbohydrates. Other grains used to make these cereals include corn, wheat, rice, and barley. Some cereals, such as Multigrain Cheerios, use a blend of grains to offer the health benefits of several staple foods at once. Although oatmeal, muesli, and cereals are great for complex carbs and fiber, itâ€™s important to know they can also be packed with extra calories from sugar. Flavored instant oatmeal, for example, can contain 15 grams of sugar per packet. Check the nutrition facts for that and other dietary information, such as which vitamins and minerals each breakfast cereal is fortified with.
