Pizza Calories

Although pizza gets a bad rap as a guilty pleasure food, it can have some nutritional value depending on the sauce and toppings. How the pizza crust is made will also affect the number of calories and overall health benefits. Using whole wheat flour instead of white flour increases the amount of complex carbs. This also increases nutrient density, and whole grains are linked to reduced risk of stroke and type 2 diabetes. What you put on top of the pizza makes the biggest difference in overall nutrition of the slice youâ€™re eating. High-calorie toppings include cheese, greasy sausage, alfredo (white) sauce, and salamis such as the ever-popular pepperoni. Pizzas with less calories are topped with lots of veggies like peppers, fresh greens like spinach, and mushrooms. Because the number of calories in pizza can vary so widely, this calorie chart should be used as a general guideline, except in the case of specific restaurant items, for example Pizza Hut.
